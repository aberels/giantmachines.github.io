Flight Club

Flight Club is the leading online and brick & mortar marketplace of highly sought after rare sneakers.

Challenge

Since launching in 2005, Flight Club’s software systems have developed by accretion and the sheer momentum of its business. Point of sale (PoS), order management, ecommerce and more had been layered on top of a FileMaker core using a tangle of vendors and services. With sales exploding and new product offerings in the works, it was clear that the systems that served the company so well during the first 10 years of growth would not support the next 10.

Flight Club engaged Giant Machines to modernize and consolidate their systems, unlock valuable product and pricing data, and rebuild their technical infrastructure to uncover efficiencies to support continued sales growth, conceptualize advanced seller’s tools, and enable future product development.

Solution

Rebuilding Flight Club’s business software is a great deal like rebuilding a bridge without stopping the flow of traffic. The “legacy” system in place is mostly working and it should be unaffected by new construction. Giant Machines ultimately delivered a new system able to work in tandem with Flight Club’s legacy software allowing for an incremental phasing in of new functionality as old functionality is replaced. This strategy allows for the business to continue to operate while being able to take advantage of efficiencies of the new system the moment they are available.

Starting with Flight Club’s core business data, we authored “extract, transform, and load” (ETL) processes that copy and synchronize it into a modern relational database. We then authored HTTP APIs to form the new foundation for software development enabling a proprietary peer-to-peer (P2P) ecommerce platform, advanced seller tools, and a mobile PoS solution.

Result

Flight Club now has a software platform that can keep pace with their growth. The APIs make it straight-forward to develop new offerings both in-house and by external vendors. The data migration to a relational database has unlocked a treasure trove of historical pricing information providing Flight Club and its customers insight into the demand and value of their products.
